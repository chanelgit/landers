## PRIMARY: Native (Taboola/Outbrain) — 10 Editorial Headlines

*Native platforms reject anything that reads as a direct ad. These are written as "story" headlines meant to bridge into the advertorial. No urgency hype, no first-person CTAs, no brand name.*

1. Why Millions of Americans Are Quietly Looking Up People They Know
2. The Public Records Most People Don't Realize Are Searchable
3. What a Simple Name Search Can Actually Reveal in 2025
4. She Wanted Answers Before a Second Date. Here's What She Found.
5. The Free Tools People Try First — And Why They Usually Fail
6. Local Parents Are Using This Method to Vet Babysitters and Coaches
7. This Is What Shows Up When You Search Your Own Name
8. The Quiet Shift: How People Screen New Contacts Before Meeting Them
9. Unknown Callers Are on the Rise — Here's How People Are Fighting Back
10. What Public Records Sites Won't Tell You Upfront

**Native headline notes:**
- Avoid "shocking," "instantly," "secret" — Taboola/Outbrain flags these
- #4 and #6 test best for CTR historically (specific scenario > generic curiosity)
- Pair headlines with lifestyle/stock imagery, NOT mugshots or blurred faces (high native rejection rate on "criminal" imagery) — save mugshot creative for the bridge page itself
- A/B test #1 vs #7 first — "self-search" framing often outperforms "search others" on cold native traffic (lower guard, higher click-through)

## Advertorial Bridge Page — Structural Note

Since lander = Advertorial Bridge Page, the native headline's job is ONLY to earn the click. The bridge page (not requested here, but flagging the handoff) should:
- Open with a first-person narrative matching headline #4 or #6 tone
- Build the case (2-3 paragraphs, "I decided to check for myself")
- Transition to soft CTA button ("See What Comes Up" / "Check Public Records") before offer page
- Match visual tone from headline → bridge → offer (don't jump from soft editorial photo to mugshot imagery, causes bounce)

### 3 Full Ad Variations (PAS Structure)

**1. Pain Point Angle**
> **Hook:** "He said he was divorced. The records said otherwise."
> **Problem:** You've been talking for weeks and something feels off — but Googling his name gets you nothing but old social media posts.
> **Agitate:** Meanwhile you're the one making yourself vulnerable, based on nothing but what he's chosen to tell you.
> **Solve/CTA:** See what's actually on public record — search a name and know before you go further. **[Check Now →]**

**2. Curiosity Angle**
> **Hook:** "Type any name. See what comes up. You won't believe what's public record."
> **Problem:** Most people assume background info is locked away in courthouses or expensive private investigator fees.
> **Agitate:** It's not — it's sitting in public databases most people never think to check, about people they interact with every day.
> **Solve/CTA:** Enter a name and see for yourself what's actually out there. **[Search Now →]**

**3. Direct Benefit Angle**
> **Hook:** "Search anyone in 60 seconds — arrests, addresses, social media, and more."
> **Problem:** Vetting someone new used to mean guessing, asking around, or hiring someone expensive.
> **Agitate:** By the time you find out the truth the slow way, it's often after something's already gone wrong.
> **Solve/CTA:** Get the full picture in under a minute, for less than a dinner out. **[Get Your Report →]**
