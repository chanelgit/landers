# Ad Copy Deliverables — Credit Builder Offer (Week 3)

**⚠️ Compliance flag before I hand this off:** Credit-related ad copy is regulated (FTC + Google Financial Services policy + FCRA implications). Anything implying a *guaranteed* score increase, "no credit check ever," or specific point gains without a substantiation disclaimer is a compliance risk — especially on Google, where financial services ads face stricter review and sometimes require certification. I've flagged copy below that needs a disclaimer (e.g., "*Results vary. See terms.*") before it goes live. Legal/compliance should sign off, not just me.

### 3 Full Ad Variations (PAS Structure)

**Ad 1 — Pain Angle**
**Hook:** "Denied again? Your credit score is costing you the apartment."

**Problem:** You found the place. You had the deposit ready. Then the application came back denied — because of a number you don't fully understand and can't seem to fix.

**Agitate:** You've tried the secured card. You've tried ignoring it. Meanwhile every apartment, every car loan, every "0% APR" ad feels like it's talking to someone else — not you.

**Solve:** This tool reports your on-time payments straight to the credit bureaus — no hard inquiry, no upfront cost to start. It's built for people rebuilding, not people who never needed to.

**CTA:** See if you qualify — takes 60 seconds. *(Results vary.)*

**Ad 2 — Curiosity Angle**
**Hook:** "I didn't pay a single collector — my score still jumped."

**Problem:** Everyone tells you the same thing: pay off collections, wait it out, hope for the best. But that's not always what actually moves the number.

**Agitate:** Credit repair companies charged $99/month and gave you a PDF of "disputes." Months later — nothing changed. You're not wrong for being skeptical.

**Solve:** There's a simpler mechanism: consistent payment history reported directly to the three bureaus, without touching your existing debt. That's the "weird trick" — it's not a trick at all.

**CTA:** See exactly how it works — free to check. *(Results vary. See terms.)*

**Ad 3 — Direct Benefit Angle**
**Hook:** "She went from a 580 to a 690 without a loan."

**Problem:** Most people think building credit means taking on more debt — a loan you don't need, a card you might not get approved for anyway.

**Agitate:** That fear keeps people stuck at a score that limits their apartment options, their car options, their peace of mind.

**Solve:** This is a credit-building tool that reports your payments monthly to Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion — no new debt, no hard pull to start, just a track record building in your favor.
